Ground Nut Oil

Cold-pressed groundnut oil includes a natural and nutty flavor of the seeds. In the cold-pressing method, groundnuts are put in between the steel or wooden crusher to extract the oil from them. Due to the lower amount of heat application, the oil retains the usual peanut essence and more nutrients than the refining process. It includes a higher smoking point, because of which they get used in deep frying.

Cocunut Oil

Production:

The coconut oil is traditionally extracted from copra. The copra can be obtained by separating the coconut meat from the whole coconut shell followed by sun drying and smoke drying for about 6 to 8 days.

Quality:

Coconut oil is 100% fat, 80-90% of which is saturated fat. This gives it a firm texture at cold or room temperatures. Fat is made up of smaller molecules called fatty acids, and there are several types of saturated fatty acids in coconut oil.

Benefits

Coconut oil has many nutrients that can contribute to your health and to a good diet. It's full of fatty acids that your body needs and may help improve cognitive function, metabolism, and hair and skin health.

Healthy for Skin:

Applying this topically has great benefits on your skin. Vitamin E level aids in the treatment of skin diseases and the control of aging. By gently applying this and allowing it to sit on dry skin overnight, it provides great nutrition and hydrates the area.
